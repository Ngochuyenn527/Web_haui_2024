A scanner can see images and converts the printed text or pictures into electronic code that can be understood by the computer. With a flatbed color scanner, the paper with the image is placed face down on a glass screen, as with photocopier. Beneath the glass are lighting and the measurement devices. Once the scanner is activated, It reads the image as a series dots and they generates as digitized image that is sent to the computer and stored as a file. The scanner operates by using three rotating lamps, each which has different colour filter: red, green and blue. The resulting three separate images are combined into one by appropriate software. A digital camera takes photos automatically and converts into digital data. It doesn’t use the film found in traditional camera, instead it has special light-sensitive silicon chip. Photographs are stored in the camera memory card before being sent to the computer. Some cameras can also be connected a printer or a TV set to make viewing images easier. This usually the case with camera phones – mobile phones with a built – in camera.
